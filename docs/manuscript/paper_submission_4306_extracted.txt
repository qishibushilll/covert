Abstract— Live streaming bullet comments are a type of public and real time text stream. They provide an open carrier for covert communication, while also introducing challenges such as text distortion, bullet comment loss, order perturbation and session synchronization difficulty. To address these issues, this paper proposes a multi carrier covert communication framework for live streaming bullet comments. A multi carrier strategy is designed to encapsulate secret message and a lightweight session advancement mechanism together with a post quantum resynchronization scheme is introduced to support continuous communication. Experimental results in both real live streaming environments and offline analysis show that the proposed method has strong resistance to interference and can achieve complete secret message transmission. Compared with existing related studies, the proposed system provides more reliable decoding performance.
Index Terms-covert communication; live streaming bullet comments; multi carrier encoding; post quantum resynchronization; continuous communication
Introduction
Live streaming bullet comments are a type of public and real time text stream. They continuously appear on live streaming or video screens in the form of short texts and exhibit the characteristics of public visibility, limited length, frequent posting, strong interactivity and temporal continuity, which make them suitable for covert channels. As a form of user interactive content, live streaming bullet comments show diverse expressive styles and can therefore serve as carriers for secret message. Meanwhile, their large volume, high posting speed and dense temporal distribution provide room for covert transmission. However, live streaming bullet comments also have evident limitations. Individual comments may be affected by platform constraints, symbol cleaning, space compression, partial loss, order perturbation and incomplete monitoring[1][2][3].
Existing research on covert communication in public environments mainly follows two approaches[1][2][4][5][6][7]. One approach constructs covert channels through time modulation, social behavior selection or posting order[1][2][6]. These methods can support secret message transmission on public platforms, but they usually depend heavily on time attributes or social behaviors, and their randomness may lead to transmission failure. The other approach realizes text steganography through natural language generation, text substitution or character level control[4][5]. These methods can change the surface form of language and reduce obvious anomalies, but their regular patterns still make them vulnerable to detection.
To address the above issues, this paper proposes a multi carrier covert communication framework for live streaming bullet comments. The framework separates the protected payload layer from the natural language bullet comment layer. At the sender side, the plaintext is first normalized, compressed, bound to the session state, encrypted, authenticated and encapsulated with commitments, and is then combined with Reed-Solomon coding and Base64URL encoding to form a protected payload[8][9]. The payload is subsequently serialized into digital sequences, organized into unified protocol packets and mapped into three types of natural bullet comments, including symbol based, punctuation based and space based comments. At the receiver side, candidate bullet comments are selected from the public bullet comment stream, after which carrier identification, packet grouping, weighted voting and protected payload reconstruction are completed at the protocol layer, followed by final decoding and integrity verification.
The main contributions of this paper are as follows.
We design a multi carrier strategy that integrates symbol based, punctuation based and space based carriers. By introducing Reed-Solomon coding and replica redundancy, the proposed framework enhances recovery capability under local cleaning, partial loss and unstable carrier conditions.
We propose a lightweight session advancement mechanism together with a periodic post quantum resynchronization mechanism. The former reduces the overhead of continuous communication by advancing the session with the existing session state and the latter provides periodic post quantum key support to enable session reconnection after message loss.

Basis of the Framework
Live Streaming Protocol and Bullet Comment Protocol
Public communication in live streaming environments mainly consists of two parts: live content transmission and bullet comment interaction. Live content transmission relies on streaming protocols for uploading, transcoding, distribution and playback, and its primary task is to ensure that audio and video content can continuously reach viewers. Bullet comment interaction, by contrast, relies on the platform’s message transmission mechanism for reception, forwarding and display, and its primary task is to support real time interaction among viewers in the live room[1][2][3].
In terms of data form, live streaming protocols handle continuous audio and video streams, whereas bullet comment protocols handle discrete text messages. The former emphasizes continuous transmission and real time playback, while the latter emphasizes message arrival, display synchronization and interactive response. Although the two types of protocols operate within the same live streaming platform environment, they differ in both processing objects and platform side priorities. Live content transmission focuses on the stable distribution of continuous media data and therefore pays more attention to stream continuity, delay control and playback consistency. Bullet comment interaction takes short text messages as the basic unit and places greater emphasis on message reception, display timing and interaction efficiency.
Since bullet comments are part of the platform’s interaction mechanism, their entry into the live room, on screen overlay and rapid refresh all possess natural business legitimacy. This public, real time and high frequency form of text interaction gives live streaming bullet comment scenarios a more distinctive interactive character than ordinary static text platforms and also provides the application background for discussing covert transmission of secret information in live streaming bullet comment environments.
Threat Model and Basic Requirements
In live streaming bullet comment scenarios, external observers mainly include ordinary viewers and platform side detection mechanisms. Ordinary viewers can see both the live streaming content and the bullet comments, but they usually regard them only as general interactive messages. The platform, by contrast, can receive, forward, filter and review bullet comments. In addition, the public bullet comment stream itself is subject to uncertainties such as partial loss, order perturbation and incomplete observation[1][2].
Beyond the external pressure brought by observers, the system must also operate under the usage constraints of a public scenario. The content of bullet comments cannot depart from the platform’s existing form of text interaction, and the sending behavior cannot deviate too obviously from the interaction rhythm of ordinary users. Otherwise, even if the messages can be delivered to the live room, abnormal appearance or abnormal behavior may still reduce the overall concealment of communication. Therefore, the relevant design must consider not only whether secret messages can be delivered, but also their naturalness in a public scenario, their robustness under local perturbations and their reliability in continuous communication.
Based on the above scenario characteristics and constraint conditions, a secret message delivery mechanism in this environment needs to satisfy three basic requirements. First, it should rely on the existing bullet comment channel of the live streaming platform to carry information in a public environment. Second, it should maintain a certain level of recovery capability under local perturbations. Third, it should preserve communication correlation across successive rounds under continuous message conditions.
Lightweight Session Advancement and Periodic PQ Resynchronization
The proposed system supports not only single secret message transmission but also continuous message communication. In this setting, the successful recovery of subsequent messages depends not only on the integrity of the current payload but also on whether the sender and receiver maintain a consistent session state. To reduce the synchronization burden in continuous communication, this paper adopts a lightweight state advancement mechanism. The basic idea is that a complete session is not reestablished between ordinary messages. Instead, the existing session is continuously advanced, which keeps the additional overhead low. However, this continuous advancement mechanism also introduces a new problem. If one or several intermediate rounds of messages are entirely lost, the receiver may lose the session state required for subsequent rounds. Even if the protocol packets have been recovered, authentication may still fail. To solve this problem, a periodic post quantum resynchronization mechanism is further introduced. The basic idea is to periodically insert a resynchronization message carrying new post quantum key encapsulation material after several rounds of ordinary messages[10]. After receiving such a message, the receiver can recover a new shared secret and reestablish the root state required for the rounds after the current one. In this way, lightweight session state advancement maintains communication continuity, while periodic post quantum resynchronization provides reconnection capability after message loss.

Conclusion
This paper proposes and implements a multi carrier covert communication framework for live streaming bullet comments, which constitute a public and real time text stream. At the sender side, secret information is covertly transmitted through protected payload construction, digital serialization, unified protocol packetization and multi carrier mapping. At the receiver side, effective recovery of the secret information is achieved through candidate filtering, packet grouping, weighted voting, payload reconstruction and final decoding. Continuous communication is further maintained through lightweight session advancement and periodic post quantum resynchronization. Experimental results show that the proposed system can stably complete decoding for both single message transmission and short window continuous message communication, and that it exhibits good recovery capability under the default lossy channel setting. The results also indicate that system performance is mainly affected by redundancy configuration, and that a redundancy level of  3 provides a relatively reasonable balance between recovery success rate and communication overhead. Under complete round loss across messages, subsequent ordinary messages may fail authentication because of state chain interruption, while subsequent resynchronization messages can reestablish session consistency. Overall, the results demonstrate that live streaming bullet comments can serve as a public text carrier for covert communication and support a covert communication framework with feasibility, recoverability and verifiability through the joint design of a multi carrier strategy and periodic post quantum resynchronization.
